This is a puppy



































A puppy is a young dog, usually under the age of 12–18 months. The term "pup" may also be used to describe a baby dog, but it can also refer to other baby animals like wolves. Here are some facts about puppies: Birth Puppies are born helpless and weigh around 8–16 oz, depending on the breed. Development Puppies grow quickly and develop their senses, with touch being the first to develop. Coat color A puppy's coat color may change as it grows. Maturity Some puppies become adults as early as one year old, while others can take up to two years. Smaller breeds tend to mature faster than larger breeds. Socialization Keeping your puppy socialized throughout its puppyhood can help it grow into a well-adjusted adult. The word "puppy" comes from the earlier word "puppie", which may have come from the Middle English words puppee or poupee, and the Old French words poupée or popée.
